Design System

of health intelligence.

Aayuv DS is an editorial-first design system for healthcare data. Every decision — from typeface to data colour — is made in service of one goal: making complex biomarker data feel trustworthy, readable, and human.

01 — Foundation

Design Principles

Seven principles that govern every visual and interaction decision in Aayuv DS. Not aspirational posters — actionable constraints used daily by design and engineering.

01
Clarity over complexity
If a chart requires explanation to understand, redesign the chart. Every visual element must earn its place by reducing cognitive load, not adding to it.
02
Narrative before numbers
Lead with the story: what does this data mean for the user? Present the conclusion first, then the evidence. Users read headlines before tables.
03
Trust through transparency
Every insight must show its source. Every prediction must show its confidence. Unexplained intelligence feels like manipulation; explained intelligence builds trust.
04
Calm visual language
Health data is inherently stressful. The UI must be the calming force — generous whitespace, restrained colour, quiet typography. Urgency through hierarchy, not saturation.
05
Scientific credibility
The system should feel closer to The Lancet than a fitness app. Clinical guidelines over lifestyle claims. Reference ranges over traffic lights.
06
Progress over perfection
Show trends, not just values. A number in isolation is meaningless. A number moving in the right direction over 12 months is a story of agency and change.
07
Editorial rhythm
The page breathes like a long-form article. Data interrupts prose; prose contextualises data. The reading experience should feel like the best data journalism.

16 — Guidelines

Voice & Tone

Aayuv DS writes like the best health journalist — not a clinician, not a wellness influencer. Precise, empathetic, direct.

✓ Write like this
"Your LDL has decreased 23% over 12 months — a meaningful shift driven by specific, identifiable changes."
"Four of your five key biomarkers are now within optimal clinical ranges."
"The goal is to push LDL below 100 mg/dL within the next two quarters."
✗ Avoid this
"Your cholesterol is HIGH! You need to take action immediately!"
"Great work! 🎉 You're on your health journey! Keep it up!"
"Your LDL value is 114 mg/dL. Normal range is 0–200 mg/dL."
PrincipleMeansExample
Lead with meaningTell user what data means, then show number"Your body is ageing 4 years slower than average."
Name the trendDescribe direction, not just current value"LDL has decreased 23% over 12 months"
Clinical, not clinical-feelingCorrect terminology explained in prose"HbA1c — a 3-month average of blood glucose"
Show the evidence chainAlways cite the guideline source"AHA/ACC threshold: <100 mg/dL"
No false urgencyReserve rose for genuinely critical signalsAmber for "monitor" · Rose for "critical"

Design System Summary
The goal is always the same: make a complex, frightening number feel understandable, actionable, and human.

Aayuv DS exists to serve that goal. Every token, every typeface, every interaction pattern was chosen not because it looks beautiful — but because it makes health data feel trustworthy. The design disappears. The insight remains.
